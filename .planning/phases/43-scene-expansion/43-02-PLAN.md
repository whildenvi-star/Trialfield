Create scene-seasonal.ts:
- Import `noise2D`, `fbm` from `./ascii-noise`
- Export `type Season = 'spring' | 'summer' | 'fall' | 'winter'`
- Export `getSeasonForMonth(month: number): Season` — month is 0-indexed (Date.getMonth()):
  - March-May (2-4) = spring
  - June-August (5-7) = summer
  - September-November (8-10) = fall
  - December-February (11, 0, 1) = winter
- Export `generateSeasonal(cols: number, rows: number, time: number): Float32Array`
  - Internally calls `getSeasonForMonth(new Date().getMonth())` to pick season
  - Each season generates a distinct visual:

  **Spring (planting):** Scattered bright dots representing seeds being dropped into soil. Horizontal rows of dots that appear sequentially from left to right (time-based offset per row). Background is very dim (soil). Small bright spots at 0.4-0.6 brightness at regular intervals along staggered rows. Use `Math.floor(time * 2) % cols` to create a "planting cursor" that sweeps across, with dots staying visible behind it.

  **Summer (growth):** Vertical lines growing upward from the bottom. Each column has a plant stem that grows from bottom to a noise-determined height. Heights increase with time (capped at 70% of rows). Add leaf-like noise clusters near stem tops. Background is dim. Brightness 0.3-0.7 for stems, 0.5-0.8 for leaf clusters.

  **Fall (harvest):** Particles falling downward. Use time-offset Y positions that wrap around. Random X positions per particle (seeded by particle index). Particles are bright (0.5-0.7). Ground layer at bottom 2 rows accumulates brightness where particles land. Creates a grain-falling visual.

  **Winter (dormant):** Very sparse, slow-moving noise field. Occasional bright point that fades in/out. fbm with low amplitude (max 0.15). A few scattered bright nodes (3-4) that pulse slowly with sin(time). Mostly dark — emphasizes negative space.

- Use unique seed offsets (200+) to differentiate from mycelium and drone.
